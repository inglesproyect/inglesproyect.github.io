History

Mompox, located in the Bolívar department, was declared a national monument in 1959 and a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1995. Founded on May 3, 1537, it is a city full of history and charm.

Attractions

Route of the Churches

Explore beautiful colonial churches throughout the city.

Historic Center

Walk through the historic streets along the Magdalena River, filled with history and charm.

Ecotourism

Enjoy the scenic beauty and biodiversity of the surroundings, including boat rides on the river.

Events

Mompox hosts vibrant events such as Holy Week celebrations and the famous Mompox Jazz Festival, featuring national and international artists.

Gastronomy

Taste the traditional dishes of Mompox, such as fish stew, cheese, and butifarras, a blend of indigenous, Spanish, and African influences.
